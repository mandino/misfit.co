Misfit Inc Development Process

Our goal with every project is to establish a standard of web development that sets the bar. That means, clean, well-managed code, advanced technology and techniques, and mobile-first development that allows the experience of what we make to be felt on any device. We will start all our builds at the markup level, so that we can compartmentalize the dev, mobile dev and testing in phases that assure quality and performance on the web.

We have frameworks for both our initial markup as well as our wordpress integration to make sure you are working with the highest level code base and code library we can offer.

Why we do it

Somewhere, there is developer who has worked day and night to meet some arbitrary client deadline. When the project began, he was excite to make something that will exist on the web. He had grand ideas, saw in his mind something he peers would envy and talk about at conferences for years. He believed he would make something that mattered. But somewhere in the process he gave in to client wants, added a ton of unnecessary features and barely had time to eek out anything at all. He was no no longer proud of it. He no longer felt as though it mattered.

Making things that matter is what makes us the envy of our peers. The projects we do and the people with whom we do them, aim to change the world. Be proud of every line of code. Be proud of every morsel of energy you put into the project in your hands. We will work hard. We will endure long nights. We will test our creative limits. Because, in the end, we are makers, we are Misfits, and what you are working on matters.

Twitter Authorization

Twitter's recent API update requires new authentication. Therefore you will need your key, consumer secret, token and user secret to use the twitter feed. Here is a quick step by step.

  1. Go to https://dev.twitter.com/apps/new and log in, if necessary
  2. Supply the necessary required fields, accept the TOS, and solve the CAPTCHA.
  3. Submit the form by clicking "Create Your Twitter Application
  4. Copy the consumer key (API key) and consumer secret and paste them into the appropriate fields in the theme options panel.
  5. To get the user token, click on the "Create My Access Token", under "Your access token" feature to automatically negotiate the access token at the permission level you need.
  6. If you recieve the green message at the top of the page indicating you have created your token, just refresh the page again.
  7. Copy and paste your Access Token and token secrets into the appropriate fields

  12. Select your page from the "Page to Create" dropdown
  13. You can configure your slider transitions
  14. Remove text transitions
  15. You can add a image (logo) overlay and remove any text
  16. Save and fist pump.

Newsletter / RSS Feature

  • Newsletter
  • Mailchimp RSS

So, What Is It?

The newsletter feature allows you to create email newsletters that look and feel like your website. The newsletter give you the option to customize a page in wordpress, add your posts and projects and then outputs a code you can copy and paste right into your email service whether it's Mailchimp, Constant Contact or any other. But, because I am a bit of a Mailchimp fan, the theme also has a Mailchimp RSS feature. The RSS Feature allows you to create a template that automatically pulls your posts based on whatever intervals you set and sends your emails out without a need to do anything on your end but write blog posts. With this template you can chose daily, weekly or monthly emails that check the number of posts that month/week/day and collects them to send out your emails.

Newsletter

  1. Login into Wordpress backend.
  2. Create Your Page Template By:
    • Click on Pages -> Add New
    • Create Your Newsletter Heading Title
    • Add any content and imagery in the content area.
    • Set a featured image as your primary photos
    • On the right hand side, choose the page template: "Newsletter Template
    • Publish
  3. On the left, click "Legend Options
  4. Click "Email Newsletter Options"
  5. Choose your options:
    • Show Page Template as Heading - You have the option to display the page content from the Template Page or keep it blank
    • Show Recent Projects. Will place your most recent 3 projects into the newsletter
    • Show Blog Posts. Will add your blog posts to the newsletter
    • Number of Blog Posts. Will determine the number of blog posts on the newsletter
  6. Save all changes
  7. Go back to "Email Newsletter Options"
  8. Under Email Newsletter Code, Preview Your Email>
  9. Go back and copy the block of code under Email Newsletter Code for your newsletter to paste into Mailchimp or any other email service.
    10. ** if you are using Mailchimp***
  10. Go to Mailchimp.com and login to your dashboard.
  11. On the left, click "Create Campaign".
  12. Choose "Regular Ol' Campaign
  13. Choose or create a list that will receive your newsletter and click NEXT
  14. Name Your campaign, subject line, reply email and add any additional tracking and social networking options you would like and click NEXT
  15. Under Design, choose "Code Your Own"
  16. Select all and paste your code into the template builder
  17. Save and name your new template
  18. Now, under Design, select "My Templates" and choose the template you just created
  19. Preview and Test
  20. If everything looks good, click NEXT
  21. Under Plain Text, click "Copy Text From HTML" and then click next.
  22. You can now schedule your email for delivery.
  23. Awww yeah!

Mailchimp RSS Feature

Check out the video on the RSS Feature using Mailchimp

  1. Login into Wordpress backend.
  2. Create Your Page Template By:
    • Click on Pages -> Add New
    • Create Your Mailchimp Newsletter Heading Title
    • Add any content and imagery in the content area.
    • On the right hand side, choose the page template: "Mailchimp RSS Template
    • Publish
  3. On the left, click "Legend Options
  4. Click "Email Newsletter Options"
  5. Under the Mailchimp RSS Email Newsletter Code, Preview Your Email>
  6. Go back and copy the block of code under Mailchimp RSS Email Newsletter Code for your newsletter to paste into Mailchimp's RSS Service.
    7. ** if you are using Mailchimp***
  7. Go to Mailchimp.com and login to your dashboard.
  8. On the left, click "Create Campaign".
  9. Choose "RSS Driven' Campaign
  10. Add your feed url, which is most likely http://yourdomain.com/feed/ or http://yourdomain.com/?feed=feed (if you have not set a permalink structure)
  11. Choose your time intervals for publication and click NEXT
  12. Choose or create a list that will receive your newsletter and click NEXT
  13. Name Your campaign, subject line, reply email and add any additional tracking and social networking options you would like and click NEXT
  14. Under Design, choose "Code Your Own"
  15. Select all and paste your code into the template builder
  16. Save and name your new template
  17. Now, under Design, select "My Templates" and choose the template you just created
  18. Preview and Test
  19. If everything looks good, click NEXT
  20. Under Plain Text, click "Copy Text From HTML" and then click next.
  21. You can now schedule your email for delivery.
